The next orb must be somewhere in that labyrinth. So get in there! But as you can see... you can't see much...
So good luck!
Well, or you could just get some light in here.

  1. Open the file
    web/shaders/tasks/vertex_lighting.vs
    in a text editor. Notice that all required attributes and uniforms are already defined in the shader.
  2. Implement ambient lighting in the shader based on the Phong lighting model.
  3. Find a way through to the next level of the labyrinth.

I guess we were lucky with the different colored walls down there. You should implement diffuse lighting next to add a little depth.

  1. Some required uniforms are not yet passed to the shader. Open the file
    web/common/GameObject.js
    and look for the comments marked with TASK.
    You will need the following WebGL functions:
    • GL.getUniformLocation()
    • GL.uniform[1234][fi][v]()
  2. Look for the comments marked with TASK and calculate the normalMatrix, transform the normals, calculate the vectors L, V and N and the attenuation factor fAtt.
  3. Then add the diffuse part of the equation.
  4. Here are some useful GLSL functions to keep in mind:
    • normalize()
    • reflect()
    • inverse()
    • transpose()
    • distance()
    • dot()
    • min()
    • max()
    • pow()

Get the orb and let's go. But which one is it? They all look the same... or do they?

  1. Look for the remaining comments marked with TASK and calculate the vector R.
  2. Then add the specular part of Phong's lighting equation.
  3. Now choose the shiny orb that looks different from the rest. When you think you've got it, click to the next task.

Those purple platforms seem conveniently placed to move to the final island. But beware, only those that have a light shining on their middle will hold you. You can't see a light shining on any of their middles? We will need fragment lighting then.

  1. Open the file
    web/common/GameObjects/LitPlatform.js
    and change the shader from
    vertexLighting
    to
    fragmentLighting
  2. Open the file
    web/common/shaders/tasks/fragmentLighting.fs
    and look for the comments marked with TASK. Note that this shader uses multiple light sources and loops over them.
  3. When your fragment lighting is correct, you will be able to see a glow in the middle of the platforms that can hold you. Take the orb and get to the last island!
